Control Mechanisms

Board Composition Strategy

Stage Board Seats Your Control
Angel (Now) 1 seat (you) 100% control
Series A 3 seats (you + 1 investor + 1 independent you choose) Effective control via independent
Series B 5 seats (you + 1 common + 2 investors + 1 independent) 2 of 5 founder-controlled

Protective Provisions (In Charter)

Option A: Super-Voting Stock (Aggressive)

  • Your shares: Class B common, 10 votes/share
  • All others: Class A common, 1 vote/share
  • You retain voting control even if ownership dilutes below 50%
  • Trade-off: Some VCs won't invest

Option B: Reserved Matters (Standard)

  • Sale of company requires your approval
  • Liquidation/dissolution requires your approval
  • Charter amendments require your approval
  • Senior securities issuance requires your approval
  • Related party transactions require your approval

Option C: Hybrid (Recommended)

  • Standard protective provisions (Option B)
  • Single-trigger acceleration on acquisition (100% vest if acquired)
  • Drag-along rights (if you sell, everyone must sell)
  • Right of first refusal (you control who becomes shareholder)

Key Insight: Board seats matter less than protective provisions. Even at 2 of 5 board seats, you maintain control of critical decisions via charter provisions.
